Biography

I am a PhD Student at the Albarqouni Lab led by Shadi Albarqouni at Helmholtz AI and the Technical University of Munich (TUM). My aim is to develop innovative deep Federated Learning algorithms for anomaly detection in medical images in a robust and privacy-preserved fashion.

I was granted a study scholarship by the e.V. Villigst and received my B.Sc. and M.Sc. in Computer Science from the Friedrich-Alexander University of Erlangen-Nuremberg (FAU), Germany, in 2015, and 2018, respectively. During my master, I did an internship abroad at the Computer Vision Centre (CVC) in Barcelona, Spain, where I developed machine learning algorithms for action recognition. I completed my master thesis at Siemens Healthineers, where I worked with Olivier Pauly, Florin C. Ghesu and Prof. Andreas K. Maier on developing novel machine learning algorithms using shared memory for medical imaging. Since then, I worked as a research engineer at Bosch Corporate Research where I developed perception and behaviour prediction machine learning algorithms for self-driving cars. My current research interests include Deep Federated Learning and Anomaly Detection.
